We talked about active listening during an interview, but it also applies to the workplace (and every other part of your life)! Active listening involves several things:
  • STOP what are you doing and face the speaker.
  • LOOK at the person speaking to you.
  • SHOW physical cues that you’ve heard the speaker (nod, say “Uh huh,” etc.).
  • REPEAT what the speaker has said to you to demonstrate that you have understood.
  • DON’T fake it! If you don’t understand, ask for clarification.

________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________ How do you make sure that what you are saying or writing is understood?
  • Make sure you KNOW what you want to say before you say it.
  • Make sure that the person listening or reading understands.
  • Look for verbal and physical cues that they understand.
  • Understand TIMING. If your supervisor is very busy, he or she may not be able to pay attention to you immediately. It may be better to wait for a time when they are not as busy.

What is work ethic?

Work ethic values include being reliable, having initiative, and a willingness to learn new skills. What are some other traits that you think go along with a good work ethic? Remember—the work that you do, no matter what you do, is a reflection of your work ethic. Some tips to develop a good work ethic include:
  • Be reliable. If you say you are going to going to do something, DO IT.
  • Double check your work. Don’t rush to complete projects.
