Lesson 15 – Interview Behavior An interview is a stressful event! Almost everyone gets nervous as an interview gets closer, but if you can manage your anxiety you will probably give a better first impression! _________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________ Relaxation Techniques:
  • Get a full night of sleep before your interview so you aren’t tired!
  • Exercise the night before or morning of your interview.
    • Even some quick stretching or walking will help!
  • Do a relaxing activity (read, take a bath).
  • Listen to calming music.
  • Laugh! The night before, watch a funny movie or spend time with a fun friend.
  • Practice relaxing breathing.
  Relaxing Breathing Techniques:
  1. Breath counting – Breathe in through your nose for 2 counts, hold the breath for 1 count, then breathe out through your mouth for 4 counts. Repeat for several minutes.
  2. Belly breathing – Place one hand on your stomach and one on your chest. Breathe in through your nose, feeling your stomach expand but your chest stay still. Breathe out through your mouth and repeat.
  3. Morning breathing – Stand up and bend forward, letting your arms hang and knees bend slightly. Inhale deeply, slowly coming into a standing position. Hold the breath for a few seconds, then exhale back into the bent position. Repeat.
  _________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________ Positive Thinking Techniques Positive thinking and visualization are techniques used by professional athletes to put themselves into a winning mindset, but the same tricks will help you succeed in any part of your life! Interview Visualization Example:
  • Sit in a comfortable position and close your eyes.
  • Take deep, calming breaths and focus on relaxing all of your muscles.
  • Sit quietly for a few minutes breathing and relaxing.
  • Begin to think about your strongest qualities and your best accomplishments.
  • Make a few positive statements about these qualities, such as “I am very organized” or “I have a perfect attendance record at school.”
  • Picture yourself in an interview, dressed nicely, doing a great job.
  • Think positive thoughts such as “I will get the job.”
  • Open your eyes and smile.
  Other Positive Thinking Tips:
  • Remember, employers understand that you’re nervous! If you are worried, you can say near the beginning of your interview “I am a little bit nervous” and they will probably understand how you are feeling.
  • The interviewers are interested in you since they offered you an interview. Therefore, they are eager to impress you too!
  • Practice smiling during your answers for confidence.
  • While you are getting ready in the morning, play music that pumps you up and makes you happy.

Things to remember on your way to the interview and in the building:
  • Your phone should be on vibrate or off (don’t use your phone!)
  • Don’t have headphones in
  • Don’t chew gum
  • Smile J
  • Be polite to everyone you encounter
