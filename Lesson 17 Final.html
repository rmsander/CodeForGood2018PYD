Brainstorming Interview Questions Take a look at your resume. From looking at your resume, can you think of any questions an employer might want to ask you? For example:
  • Why did you leave your last job?
  • What did you do in between…
    • Leaving school and beginning work?
    • Leaving school and now?
    • Your past job and current job?
  • What was your greatest accomplishment at your last job?
  • How would you use your skills at this job?
  • Tell me more about the volunteer work you did.
Try to think of a couple more based off of your resume! _________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________ Are there questions that interviewers aren’t allowed to ask you? Yes! By law, you should not be asked anything that reveals your:
  • Age
  • Race
  • Gender
  • Religion
  • National Origin
  • Sexual Orientation
  • Marital Status
  • Health
If you are asked any questions about these subjects, you do not have to answer! Instead, you could say: “I’m sorry, but I prefer not to answer that.” “I am not comfortable answering that question.” Illegal Question Examples: There are many questions that employers are not allowed to ask, such as:
  • “What is your maiden name?”
  • “What is your date of birth?”
  • “What is your husband/wife’s profession?”
  • “Do you have any disabilities that would prevent you from performing this job?”
  • “Do you have health issues that would prevent you from performing this job?”
  • “Have you ever been hospitalized?”
Employers CANNOT ask about your disabilities and health issues until after they have made you a conditional job offer. However, they may ask if you are capable of performing the job requirements.
